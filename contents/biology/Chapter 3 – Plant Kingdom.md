## üî• Introduction
- Whittaker‚Äôs (1969) Five Kingdoms: Monera, Protista, Fungi, Plantae, Animalia.  
- Kingdom **Plantae** includes: **Algae, Bryophytes, Pteridophytes, Gymnosperms, Angiosperms**.  
- Earlier: Fungi, Monerans, Protists with cell walls were included ‚Üí now excluded.  
- Cyanobacteria ‚â† Algae anymore.  

### üå± Evolution of Classification
1. **Artificial system** (Linnaeus) ‚Üí Based on superficial characters (habit, leaf, colour).  
   - Weakness: separated related species.  
2. **Natural system** (Bentham & Hooker) ‚Üí Based on natural affinities, anatomy, embryology, chemistry.  
3. **Phylogenetic system** ‚Üí Based on evolutionary relationships.  
4. **Modern approaches**:  
   - **Numerical taxonomy** (computer-based, all characters equally weighted).  
   - **Cytotaxonomy** (chromosome number, structure).  
   - **Chemotaxonomy** (chemical composition).

## üåä 3.1 Algae
- Chlorophyll-bearing, simple, thalloid, autotrophic, mostly aquatic.  
- Habitats: Freshwater, marine, moist soils, wood, lichens (with fungi), symbiotic (sloth bear).  
- Forms:  
  - Colonial (Volvox)  
  - Filamentous (Spirogyra, Ulothrix)  
  - Large kelps (100 m).  

### üå± Reproduction
- **Vegetative** ‚Üí Fragmentation.  
- **Asexual** ‚Üí Spores (zoospores ‚Üí flagellated).  
- **Sexual** ‚Üí Gametic fusion:  
  - Isogamy (Ulothrix, Spirogyra).  
  - Anisogamy (Eudorina).  
  - Oogamy (Volvox, Fucus).  

### üåç Economic Importance
- Fix ~50% CO‚ÇÇ by photosynthesis.  
- Release O‚ÇÇ; primary producers.  
- Food: Porphyra, Laminaria, Sargassum.  
- Hydrocolloids: Algin (brown), Carrageenan (red).  
- Agar (Gelidium, Gracilaria): culture media, ice-cream, jellies.  
- Chlorella: rich in protein, food supplement (even in space).  

### üåø Classes of Algae
| Class         | Pigments                         | Food Reserve        | Cell Wall     | Flagella                 | Habitat |
|---------------|----------------------------------|---------------------|---------------|--------------------------|---------|
| Chlorophyceae (Green) | Chlorophyll a, b              | Starch (pyrenoids)  | Cellulose     | 2‚Äì8, equal, apical       | Fresh/brackish/salt |
| Phaeophyceae (Brown)  | Chlorophyll a, c, fucoxanthin | Laminarin, mannitol | Cellulose + algin | 2, unequal, lateral      | Marine mostly |
| Rhodophyceae (Red)    | Chlorophyll a, d, phycoerythrin | Floridean starch   | Cellulose, pectin, polysulphates | Absent | Mostly marine |

**Examples**:  
- Green: Chlamydomonas, Volvox, Ulothrix, Spirogyra, Chara.  
- Brown: Ectocarpus, Laminaria, Fucus, Sargassum.  
- Red: Polysiphonia, Porphyra, Gracilaria, Gelidium.

## üå± 3.2 Bryophytes
- ‚ÄúAmphibians of plant kingdom‚Äù ‚Üí live on soil but need water for sexual reproduction.  
- Habitat: moist, shaded places.  
- Ecological role:  
  - Pioneer species in succession.  
  - Decompose rocks ‚Üí soil formation.  
  - Prevent soil erosion (dense mats).  
  - Peat moss (Sphagnum): fuel, packaging.  

### General Features
- Plant body: thalloid, attached by rhizoids (unicellular/multicellular).  
- No true roots, stems, leaves (but appear similar).  
- Main plant body = **Gametophyte (haploid)**.  
- Sex organs:  
  - Male = **Antheridium** (biflagellate antherozoids).  
  - Female = **Archegonium** (flask-shaped, produces egg).  
- Fertilisation ‚Üí Zygote ‚Üí Sporophyte (foot, seta, capsule).  
- Sporophyte dependent on gametophyte.  
- Spores (haploid) by meiosis ‚Üí new gametophyte.  

### üü¢ Liverworts
- Habitat: moist, shady (stream banks, damp soil, tree bark).  
- Plant body = dorsiventral thallus (e.g., Marchantia).  
- Asexual: Fragmentation, Gemmae in gemma cups.  
- Sexual: Antheridia & Archegonia on same/different thalli.  
- Sporophyte: foot, seta, capsule ‚Üí spores (meiosis).  

### üü¢ Mosses
- Dominant phase = gametophyte.  
- Two stages:  
  1. **Protonema** ‚Üí green, filamentous, first stage.  
  2. **Leafy stage** ‚Üí arises from protonema buds, has stems & leaves.  
- Reproduction:  
  - Vegetative ‚Üí fragmentation, budding in protonema.  
  - Sexual ‚Üí Archegonia + Antheridia at shoot apex.  
- Sporophyte = foot, seta, capsule (with spores).  
- Examples: Funaria, Polytrichum, Sphagnum.

## üåø 3.3 Pteridophytes
- First **vascular land plants** (xylem + phloem).  
- Includes ferns, horsetails.  
- Habitat: cool, damp, shady; some in sandy soils.  
- Economic importance: ornamentals, medicinal, soil-binders.  

### General Features
- Dominant phase = **Sporophyte** (true roots, stem, leaves).  
- Leaves:  
  - Microphylls (small, Selaginella).  
  - Macrophylls (large, ferns).  
- Sporangia on **sporophylls**; sometimes form **strobili (cones)**.  
- Spores (meiosis) ‚Üí Prothallus (small, photosynthetic gametophyte).  
- Water needed for fertilisation.  

### Types
- **Homosporous** ‚Üí 1 type of spore (majority).  
- **Heterosporous** ‚Üí 2 types: microspores (male), megaspores (female).  
  - Selaginella, Salvinia.  
  - Female gametophyte retained ‚Üí precursor of seed habit.  

### Classes
1. Psilopsida (Psilotum)  
2. Lycopsida (Selaginella, Lycopodium)  
3. Sphenopsida (Equisetum)  
4. Pteropsida (Pteris, Adiantum, Dryopteris)

## üå≤ 3.4 Gymnosperms
- Naked seed plants (ovules not enclosed in ovary).  
- Includes medium/large trees, shrubs (e.g., Cycas, Pinus, Cedrus, Ginkgo).  
- Tallest: Sequoia (redwood).  

### Morphology
- Roots: tap root.  
  - Mycorrhiza (Pinus).  
  - Coralloid roots with N‚ÇÇ-fixing cyanobacteria (Cycas).  
- Stem: branched (Pinus) / unbranched (Cycas).  
- Leaves:  
  - Pinnate (Cycas), persistent.  
  - Needle-like (conifers): xerophytic features (cuticle, sunken stomata).  

### Reproduction
- **Heterosporous** (microspores + megaspores).  
- Microsporophylls (male strobili) ‚Üí microspores ‚Üí pollen grains (male gametophyte).  
- Megasporophylls (female strobili) ‚Üí ovules ‚Üí megaspores ‚Üí female gametophyte with archegonia.  
- Fertilisation: Pollen carried by air ‚Üí pollen tube ‚Üí sperm to egg.  
- Seed: Naked, not enclosed by fruit.  

---

## üå∏ 3.5 Angiosperms
- Flowering plants.  
- Seeds enclosed in **fruits**.  
- Huge diversity: from Wolffia (smallest) to Eucalyptus (>100 m).  
- Provide food, fodder, fuel, medicines, timber.  
- **Classes**:  
  - Dicots (two cotyledons, e.g., sunflower, mango).  
  - Monocots (one cotyledon, e.g., wheat, maize, grass).  

---

## üìå Summary
- **Algae**: Chlorophyll-bearing, aquatic; 3 classes ‚Üí Chlorophyceae, Phaeophyceae, Rhodophyceae.  
- **Bryophytes**: Amphibians of plant kingdom; gametophyte dominant; includes mosses & liverworts.  
- **Pteridophytes**: First vascular plants; sporophyte dominant; some heterosporous.  
- **Gymnosperms**: Naked-seeded; cones; no fruit.  
- **Angiosperms**: Flowering plants; seeds in fruits; divided into dicots & monocots.

## üìù NCERT Exercise Questions with Answers

**Q1. What is the basis of classification of algae?**  
üëâ Based on: (i) Pigments, (ii) Storage products, (iii) Cell wall composition, (iv) Flagella type.  

**Q2. When and where does reduction division take place in the life cycle of a liverwort, a moss, a fern, a gymnosperm and an angiosperm?**  
üëâ  
- Liverwort ‚Üí in sporophyte capsule during spore formation.  
- Moss ‚Üí sporophyte capsule during spore formation.  
- Fern ‚Üí sporangia of sporophyte.  
- Gymnosperm ‚Üí microsporangia (pollen mother cell) & megasporangia (megasporocyte).  
- Angiosperm ‚Üí pollen mother cell (anther) & megaspore mother cell (ovule).  

**Q3. Name three groups of plants that bear archegonia. Briefly describe the life cycle of any one.**  
üëâ Groups: Bryophytes, Pteridophytes, Gymnosperms.  
Example: **Moss (Bryophyte)** ‚Üí Dominant gametophyte ‚Üí produces archegonia & antheridia ‚Üí fertilisation in water ‚Üí sporophyte dependent on gametophyte ‚Üí spores ‚Üí new gametophyte.  

**Q4. Mention the ploidy of the following:**  
- Protonemal cell of moss ‚Üí Haploid (n).  
- Primary endosperm nucleus (dicot) ‚Üí Triploid (3n).  
- Leaf cell of moss ‚Üí Haploid (n).  
- Prothallus cell of fern ‚Üí Haploid (n).  
- Gemma cell in Marchantia ‚Üí Haploid (n).  
- Meristem cell of monocot ‚Üí Diploid (2n).  
- Ovum of liverwort ‚Üí Haploid (n).  
- Zygote of fern ‚Üí Diploid (2n).  

**Q5. Write a note on economic importance of algae and gymnosperms.**  
üëâ  
- **Algae**:  
  - Fix CO‚ÇÇ, produce O‚ÇÇ.  
  - Food (Porphyra, Laminaria).  
  - Hydrocolloids (algin, carrageenan).  
  - Agar (culture media, food).  
  - Chlorella ‚Üí protein-rich supplement.  
- **Gymnosperms**:  
  - Timber (Pinus, Cedrus).  
  - Paper (conifers).  
  - Ephedra ‚Üí medicinal (ephedrine).  
  - Ornamental (Cycas).  

**Q6. Both gymnosperms and angiosperms bear seeds, then why are they classified separately?**  
üëâ Gymnosperms = naked seeds (no fruit), ovules exposed.  
Angiosperms = seeds enclosed in fruits, ovules protected in ovary.  

**Q7. What is heterospory? Comment on significance. Give examples.**  
üëâ Production of two types of spores: microspores (male), megaspores (female).  
- Found in Selaginella, Salvinia, Gymnosperms, Angiosperms.  
- Significance: Precursors to seed habit ‚Üí evolutionarily important.  

**Q8. Explain terms with examples:**  
- Protonema ‚Üí Filamentous first stage in moss (*Funaria*).  
- Antheridium ‚Üí Male sex organ (Bryophytes, Pteridophytes).  
- Archegonium ‚Üí Female sex organ (Bryophytes, Pteridophytes, Gymnosperms).  
- Diplontic ‚Üí Life cycle with diploid sporophyte dominant (Gymnosperms, Angiosperms).  
- Sporophyll ‚Üí Leaf-like structure bearing sporangia (ferns, Pinus cones).  
- Isogamy ‚Üí Fusion of similar gametes (Spirogyra).
